Minecraft

Minecraft stands out not only for the way it inspires me creatively, but also because of its unique aesthetic. Look, I know the visuals look dated and a bit silly, but few games have visuals so endearing and charming. I know I'm not the only one who feels that way either, or else Minecraft's graphics wouldn't be so iconic.

Genshin Impact

Genshin Impact is light on story and big on exploration, encouraging players to peek under every rock, glide from every precipice, and bash through every camp of goblins. This free-to-play, open-world action/RPG is an incredible amalgamation of stylish art and sound, easy-to-learn combat, and magical adventure around every corner.

Valorant

Overall, the addicting and often stressful gameplay loop coupled with the unique agents and their abilities makes for a fresh experience every time. The agents' abilities are definitely the highlight here though, adding a ton of versatility where you can come up with your own strategies for crafty plays.

Stardew Valley

There’s a comfort in Stardew Valley that I’ve rarely found in other games. The music that changes with each season perfectly fits the mellowness of the 16-bit countryside town, and all the activities that await offer both peace in my daily outines and excitement in the land’s mysteries.
